Wrathion

niseko

Fight Mechanics

Wrathion is a 2 phase encounter that transitions between phase 1 and phase 2, separated by intermissions. On Mythic, players immediately start with Creeping Madness which will hinder the ability to move throughout the fight. You can safely stand still for most of Wrathion's abilities as a ranged, and even be far enough away so that Gale Blast will not affect you like it does the melee. Every so often he will cast Incineration on 3 random allies who need to run a bit away from the group to reduce the amount of damage you take. There are 2 Incineration casts per phase, and each phase is separated by about 3 minutes, so you can safely use raid-wide damage reductions like Spirit Link Totem on these abilities if you want. Occasionally the boss will cast Burning Cataclysm and you will need to move to the opposite side of the platform as his 'smoke cloud'. This is a great time to use Wind Rush Totem to allow the slower players to be able to reach the safe area in time. You will repeat these steps again before the boss transitions into his next phase.

In Phase 2, three players will need to grab the Scales of Wrathion from under the boss and run through as many Crackling Shards as they can so the group can kill off as many as possible. Help kill some of the Crackling Shards that are far away from the boss so that the melee don't accidentally gain stacks from running back to Wrathion if he transitions back into Phase 1.

Suggested Talents

A pretty standard talent setup is great for Wrathion, with the addition of Wind Rush Totem to help move your group during the intermissions. Earthen Wall Totem is quite good to place while everyone is stacked up, and sometimes can be useful during the intermissions when everyone is in the 'safe' area.

Notable Azerite Traits and Essences

A number of Azerite and Essence combinations are more than fine for Wrathion. Overflowing Shores might be a nice pick if you can take one as sometimes people are forced to remain still even if they're spread out a bit because of Creeping Madness. This fight does not have a lot of damage going out, so feel free to experiment with some popular essences, such as Vision of Perfection or Worldvein Resonance majors.

Healing Tips

  • Incineration deals predictable raid-wide damage on a set timer, so you can easily prep a good Cloudburst Totem for it by dropping your totem about 5 seconds before the boss casts an Incineration.
  • Some healers such as Discipline Priests have a hard time healing the intermissions because they don't have a target to attack, so using Healing Tide Totem during the intermissions may help out your group.
  • Make sure you place down Wind Rush Totem before the boss teleports away so that your teammates can gain the movement speed before they run to the safe area.
  • Earth Elemental may be useful during Phase 2 to 'tank' the Ashwalker Assassins that spawn if your tanks are unable to pick them up right away.
